## Key Insights from the Conversation

### 1. "Solve Your Own Problem First"
Don't build for a hypothetical customer. Build for yourself. You understand the problem intimately. You can validate if the solution actually works. Then sell to others like you.

### 2. "Domain Expertise is Your Moat"
Generic SaaS tools (Salesforce, HubSpot) are horizontal. Industry-specific beats generic every time because you understand the nuances, language, and workflows.

### 3. "The Tools Are Your MVP"
The customer intelligence dashboard, territory planner, etc. aren't just examples - they're your minimum viable product. Start using them. Prove value. Then productize.

### 4. "Bootstrap vs. Venture: Both Work"
- **Venture path:** Raise $1-3M, grow fast, exit for $150-400M
- **Bootstrap path:** Stay lean, grow profitably, $3-5M/year + $75-150M exit
Both create wealth. Choose based on your goals and risk tolerance.

### 5. "You Don't Need to Be Technical"
Claude Code democratizes building. If you can describe what you need, you can build it. The limiting factor is domain expertise and entrepreneurial mindset, not coding ability.
